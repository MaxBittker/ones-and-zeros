About

Ones and Zeros is a tech space for people who don't normally see themselves in tech. We introduce electronics and basic coding through workshops and classes with a critical lens and a focus on creativity. Our hands-on events run in Toronto, Ottawa, and Kingston, and we try hard to make them accessible and affordable. The Recurse Center and the School For Poetic Computation give us endless inpsiration.

People

Phil Schleihauf – director

An electronic artist and musician challenging cultural norms and narratives and baking bread. And teaching! Phil started Ones and Zeros after attending the School For Poetic Computation in 2018.

Eren Suner – teaching assistant

Hey, I am Eren, a tech enthusiast and a full-time learner. I’ve created workshops for visually-impaired folks and refugees to make tech more accesible at YGA.

Angela Schleihauf – board

Angela Schleihauf is a musician, composer and arts administrator. As the Project Manager for Ottawa Symphony's 3D StringTheory, she helps facilitate the creation of 3D printed instruments, design challenges, and a video series documenting the project. In her performing life, she can be found in wide range of genres and multi-discipline collaborations including improvised music, music for theatre, classical ensembles, and original folk/pop bands. She has toured in Canada, Sweden, and South Korea and enjoys converting daily experiences into music.

Asad Chishti – board

Asad Chishti is an inventor at Chairs and Tables and the Assistant Director at Adjacent Furniture. He biked across Turtle Island i.e. Canada (x2) to better understand Happiness (and histories), spent a year researching Home, investigated Health for four seasons and is currently trying to "make an apple pie from scratch".
